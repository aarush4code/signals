Signals in the past

Smoke Signals

Native americans used smoke signals to cummunicate long distances across the great plains. They signalled this way to mainly warn others of a threat. They achived this by adding thick vegatation, to get thick smoke. They even had a system that conved the seriousness of the danger. One puff of smoke mant that they were ok, 2 puffs meant, they were ok, but expecting an attack, and three meant imidiate danger.

Torches

Smoke signals worked great during the day, but were hard too see at night. Thats why people used torches to communitcate after dark. They mainly used them on forts to send pre decided messages down to the people on the ground. One torch might be signalling the all clear, two might signal that they were enemies approching, and waving torches might signal that they were under an attack.

Whistles

Near Spain, people used to use whitles to communicate overlong distances,and some whitles can go as far as 3 miles. The terrain there is very hilly and has mountains so whitsles are a great way to communicate. The whistles can even signal specific messages, as its closly related to the language their. Some schools in Spain still teach the language to keep the tradition alive.

Drums and Other Muscial Instruments

In central or West Africa people used to use drums to communicate from town to town. Drums were a large part of their culture, so it was very common to use one. The rhythms played by the drums represtented words of their language, so a highlly skilled drummer could send entire sentences across. Other instruments such as trumpets were used in war to send messages across the battle field. The messages that were sent were normally battle commands telling soilders to attack or retreat.

Cave Paintings

During the Paleolithic era people painted images on walls to represent different things. These drawings have been found all over the world in places like Spain and France. They painted these sybols with their hands on cave walls and it usually represtentedthings such as hunting information and it also marked down territories for their group.
