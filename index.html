Signals

What is a Signal

A signal can be anything from a high 5 to a call sign in football. It can even be a smoke sign. They are a way of sending a message, which can be as specific or broad as you want. Overall, signals are a way to tell someone or something information, and are essential to our every day life.

Signals from the past

People have been using signals since they lived in caves. Earlier the signals they used were very simple, and only communicated basic nessecities, such as food, danger, shelter, cordinating a location, or even social bonding.

Today the main way people use signals is with technology, but other non-technichal signals are still commonly used. The most common signals still convey the same messages that signals 30,000-40,000 years ago did.

Animals and Signals

Humans aren't the only species who use signals. Animals use them all the time too. They use them in a similar fashion to communicate a wide variety of subjects. For example, they can use signals to communicate, find food and warn others of danger.

Plants also use signals, though they use them very differently than humans and animals do. They use signals to communicate with their roots and some types of plants even react in a certain way when provoked.
