Signals Today

Football Call Signs

In lots of sports, not just football, a team has call signs that symbolise their strategy for the play. These signs are usually signaled to the player before the play and in breaks between the game. these call signs can be verbal, like, "Blue42", "Omaha", and "Green 18". But can also be actions that the QB or the coach does to his players like the QB taping his helmet or lifting his leg.

Ref's Whistle

In mostly team sports, the ref will blow a whistle to signal for breaks and the end of a game. This is pretty iconic as refs have been whistling since the games were invented. The whistle they use is important because if it isn't loud enough players might not be able to here it over the noisy crowd.

Taffic Lights

Traffic lights are essential to our every day life as they make sure everyone is safe on the road, without them they would be way to many accidents and every turn would be a hazard. Ther're three colors on the light, red on the very top, signals for the drivers to stop. Green In the middle tells them to go and yellow tells them to slow down.

T.V Remote

T.V remotes let us "talk" to the T.V by sending infared rays towards the T.V. The T.V then picks up these rays. Each button on the remote has a different infared ray, so the T.V knows whether it should lower or if it should higher the volume. Some of the newer remotes use radio frequency instead, which lets the remote us bluetooth or wifi to connect instead. The bennifit of using this is that it will still work if something is between it and the T.V.

Emergency Sirens

To get an urgent message to many people quickly, the police might use an emergency signal to get their attention. These signals can be for many things, like a police or ambulence siren, a tornado, flood and can even signal for a missing child. Police and ambulence sirens are mainly used on the road to get people to clear. Flood, tornado and a missing child alert, or a amber alert is a message sent to everybodies phone and flood and tornado sirens are also played on loud speakers.
