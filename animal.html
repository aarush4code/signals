How Animals use Signals

Dogs/Wolves

Dogs and wolves both signals in many ways. A wolf sticking their tail up, means that the are asserting dominance within the pack and/or are challenging the alpha. On the other hand, having their tail down or tucked in between their legs in a sign of surrender. Another thing wolves are know for is howling. Howling is a way wolves assemble the pack together. Dogs, their domesticated counterpart, can be trained to respond to signals given by humans. They can be trained to roll over, jump, sit, follow their owner and even can be medical or service dogs.

Bees

Bees use signals to communicate where food is. They communicate this by doing special dances. One dance that bees perform is called the waggle dance. The wangle dance usually performed by honeybees indicates, where the food is. The direction of the waggle conveys the direction of the food sorce and the speed and duration conveys the distance and the quallity.

Elephants

Elephants are another animal that uses signals. They use them mainly for communication, as their territories can span much over a million square acers. The main way they send each other signals is by using their trunks, which can be heard from as far as six miles. For farther signals though, an elephant can stomp their feet into the ground to send out a low frequency vibration that can be picked up by other elephants. An elephants stomp can go as far as twenty miles.

Meercats

A meercats main use of signals is to warn other meercats in the clan of danger. Meercats even have different types of calls for different threats. For instance if their is an attack from above by a bird a meercat might make a high pitched sound, but if threatened by a jackel or a snake, might emit a lower pitched one. They even have a special click noise symbolizing the all clear when the threat is gone.

Bats

Because bats are nocturtal, which means that they are alseep during the dat and up at night. They need a way to see in the dark. Thats why they use echolocation. Echolocation is when they emit a high frequency sound that bounces of their surrondings. After it bounces off the surrondings, it returns back to them and the bats know whats around them. They mainly use this to hunt, navigate and avoid obsticles in the dark.

Dolphines and Whales

Signals are not just limited to to land animals. Sea creatures use them all the time too. Take dolpines or whales as an example. Dolphines use a series of clicks and whistles to communicate and each dolphine is even givin their own didtict name. They also use echoloction to locate prey when its dark. Whales also use signals to communicate. Humpback whales have their own songs which can reach others up to 500 miles away. They too use echolocation to find prey. Sperm whales also use a click sound to communicate. Their clicks are so loud that they can reach up to 230 decibells. Thats as loud as a jet engine.

Anglerfish

Anglerfish live deep in the ocean where almost no light reaches. Its way of signalling is by flashing a light thats dangling of the top of his head, right in front of his mouth. It uses signals to catch prey, by tricking smaller fish to come closer to the light and then eating it. It uses bioluminescents which is when an animal uses chemicalls to produce light inside its body, like a glow stick.
